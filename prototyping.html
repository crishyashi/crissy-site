PROTOTYPING

Overview:

A group of UC Berkeley students set to tackle the problem of how to improve and maintain mental health among students at the university. A three-month long open-ended exploration composed of literary research, multiple user interviews, and usability testing resulted in the development of a digital prototype for an appointment booking app for stress relieving activities.

Goal

Develop an intuitive, user-friendly, and practical prototype by learning how users will naturally interact with the app.

Process:

Iteration 1:

During the early stages of the design, we had two initial concepts that we wanted to test out. I developed sketches of each concept on poster paper and highlighted the key features and benefits. These sketches were shared with the class and feedback was gathered from fellow classmates.

Iteration 2:

After narrowing down the concepts, I decided to develop a low-fidelity paper prototype in order to quickly test the product idea and illustrate the user interactions. I created an iPhone using cardboard and sketched the different screens of the app on paper. I tested the prototype with users by manually switching the screens based on what the user selected. I observed how the users interacted with the prototype and gathered feedback during the walkthroughs.

Iteration 3:

Based off of feedback we received during Iteration 1, I worked with one other teammate to develop a digital layout of the app using Adobe Illustrator.

Final Prototype:

In order to demonstrate the app flow, we used Figma to develop our final prototype. The text information was now interactive, giving users a more realistic experience of the product.

Output:

The final design concept was shared at the 2017 Jacobs Winter Design Showcase at UC Berkeley.
